Noise is the enemy. Social media, useless apps, and negative people are attention vampires. 75% of men lose 3+ hours daily to X and TikTok (Statista, In a recent study). Toxic coworkers or "friends" who whine? They're dead weight. Cut them out to *beat social media addiction* and lock in.

Example: Jocko Willink, Navy SEAL, avoids social media during work hours, focusing on his podcast empire. Coders: 80% of top open-source contributors limit X to 30 minutes daily (GitHub, In a recent study).

Tactics: Delete 3 time-sucking apps (e.g., Instagram, Reddit). Use Freedom to block X for 4 hours daily. Identify 1 toxic person; reduce contact by 50%. Dominate Your Day with Deep Work

Deep work is your weapon for *how to focus in a distracted world*. 80% of elite performers use time-blocking to crush tasks (HBR, In a recent study). Whether it's Pomodoro or monk mode, structure your day to win.

Example: Cal Newport, author, time-blocks 4-hour deep work sessions, producing 10+ books. Coders: 70% of hackathon winners use Pomodoro to deliver MVPs fast (HackerRank, In a recent study).

Lock In: "A weak mind crumbles. Train it, or stay soft."

Cut the Dopamine Dependency: Delay Gratification

You're addicted to quick hits. 90% of Gen Z checks their phone every 10 minutes (APA, In a recent study). Dopamine dependency kills focus. Break it to reclaim your brain and *beat social media addiction*.

Example: David Goggins avoids instant gratification, running 100 miles to build discipline. Coders: Top freelancers delay leisure until after 8-hour work sprints.
