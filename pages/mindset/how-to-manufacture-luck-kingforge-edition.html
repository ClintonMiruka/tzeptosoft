Luck isn't a fairy godmother; it's a system you can hack. Random chance_your birthplace, family, or IQ_is out of your control. But manufactured luck? The concept of luck has been misunderstood for centuries. People look at successful individuals and attribute their achievements to "being in the right place at the right time" or "having connections." While these factors play a role, they're not the complete picture. True luck is what happens when preparation meets opportunity_but even that definition is incomplete. Real manufactured luck is when you create opportunities through deliberate action, then have the skills and mindset to capitalize on them when they appear.

Consider this: In any given field, there are thousands of opportunities circulating at any moment. Most people never see them because they're not looking in the right places or don't recognize them when they appear. The "lucky" person has trained themselves to spot patterns, make connections others miss, and take calculated risks that position them advantageously. This isn't magic_it's a skill you can develop through the principles outlined in this manifesto.

1. Manufacture Luck by Being Bold ‚ö°

Boldness has genius! Bold men of action rule the world. Rebels become kings because they dare to defy. Entrepreneurs like Elon Musk or Steve Jobs reshaped history by taking risks. Sitting still? Your odds of winning are zero. Acting boldly? Your odds are always higher than zero. Boldness isn't reckless_it's calculated risk. The beauty? Failure often costs nothing. Send a resume: you either get the job or stay where you are. Ask someone out: they say yes, or you're no worse off. You miss every shot you don't take.

Boldness operates on a simple mathematical principle: probability multiplication. When you take zero actions, your probability of success is exactly zero. When you take one bold action, even if it has only a 5% chance of success, you've moved from zero to five percent. Take ten such actions, and your cumulative probability increases dramatically. This is why serial entrepreneurs often succeed_they're constantly launching new ventures, knowing that each one has a chance, and the law of averages works in their favor over time.

Why It Works: Boldness expands your comfort zone. Each risk trains your brain to handle uncertainty, opening doors others fear to touch. Lady Fortuna loves the brave and despises cowards. 2. Manufacture Luck by Being Persistent üìä

Life's a numbers game. Internet marketers know: enough shots, and someone bites. Traffic √ó Conversion Rate = Success. Can't land a date? Ask one person, and it's 50/50. Ask 100, and your odds skyrocket. Failure doesn't hurt? Keep swinging. The difference between chess (deterministic) and life (probabilistic) is simple: life lets you draw again. If your "traffic" (effort) is high but results are low, tweak your offer or audience. Sell fish at the lake, not the pool.

The mathematics of persistence are undeniable. In probability theory, independent events multiply. If you have a 10% chance of success with each attempt, your chance of at least one success after ten attempts isn't 10%_it's approximately 65%. After twenty attempts, it rises to about 88%. This exponential increase in probability with repeated attempts is why persistence is so powerful. Most people quit after two or three tries, never reaching the critical mass where success becomes statistically likely.

Why It Works: Persistence beats bad luck. Each attempt refines your approach, increasing your conversion rate. Most quit after one try_you won't. Pro Tip: Track your efforts (applications, pitches) to spot patterns and improve.

3. Manufacture Luck by Unique Action üöÄ

Ordinary paths lead to ordinary results. Extraordinary luck finds those on extraordinary journeys. Steve Jobs didn't follow the crowd_he sold computers to the masses, a radical move at the time. Unique action has no blueprint, but it's your ticket to outsized rewards. Read Zero to One by Peter Thiel for inspiration on building something new. Dare to zig when others zag, and luck will follow.

Unique action creates what economists call "economic moats"_competitive advantages that are difficult to replicate. When you're doing something truly different, you're not competing on price or features; you're creating your own category where you're the only option. This positioning attracts opportunities that simply don't exist in crowded markets. The "luck" that seems to follow innovators isn't random_it's the natural result of being in an uncontested space where any positive outcome appears disproportionately large compared to efforts.

Why It Works: Unique action puts you in rare territory where competition is low and opportunities are high. You're not fighting for scraps_you're creating the feast. Pro Tip: Start small_test a niche idea or side project.

  • Example: Mike launched a blog on crypto in 2017 when it was niche. Now has 50k readers and a 6-figure income. Vibe: Visionary. üåü
  • Tactic: Brainstorm one unique project idea this week (e.g., a niche YouTube channel). üí°

A strong man doesn't need luck_that's why he gets it.

Preparation transforms serendipity into strategy. When you've built a broad base of knowledge and skills, you can connect dots that others don't even see. By expanding your internal capabilities (skills, knowledge, networks), you increase the surface area where opportunities can intersect with your life.

Why It Works: Preparation turns chance into inevitability. A prepared king can't be stopped. Pro Tip: Always have a "next step" ready_skills, savings, or contacts_for when opportunity knocks.
