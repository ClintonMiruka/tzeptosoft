Chapter 1: The Great-Souled Man: Aristotle’s Code of Reciprocity

Aristotle’s “great-souled man” in *Nicomachean Ethics* embodies *treat people how they treat you*. He aids willingly but scorns begging, humbles the mighty, and respects the modest.

The Code: 70% of men feel disrespected in social interactions (Psychology Today, 2025). Reciprocity aligns with Aristotle’s principle: mirror behavior to maintain dignity.

The Why: Being kind to the cruel signals weakness, while disrespecting the humble reveals vulgarity. Reciprocity preserves honor.

Mental Model: Tit-for-Tat: Mirror actions to reward cooperation and punish hostility, ensuring fair interactions.

“It is characteristic of the great-souled man to be haughty towards men of position, but courteous towards those of moderate station.” — Aristotle
